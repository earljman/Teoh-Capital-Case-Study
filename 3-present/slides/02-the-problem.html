Pain framing

Three margin killers drain mid-market warehouses: picking labor, retail chargebacks, and DIM freight

Mid-market operators hit a headcount ceiling Like adding more waiters to a tiny kitchen — you can't serve more meals, you just bump into each other. Order volume grows, but lines picked per hour flatlines. : they add pickers, but lines-per-hour flatlines because legacy WMS can't optimize the floor. Picking consumes most labor spend — and half the shift is walking. Meanwhile retail chargebacks Ship to Walmart or Target with the wrong label, late ASN, or bad pallet setup and they automatically deduct money from your invoice — like losing points on a test for formatting errors. and DIM freight penalties Carriers bill for box size, not just weight. Ship a small item in a big box and you pay to ship the empty air inside it. erode margin on every channel.

50–70%
Picking share of labor cost
Most of what you pay warehouse workers goes to pickers walking aisles — not supervisors, receivers, or packers.
2–5%
Wholesale revenue lost to chargebacks
For every $100 you sell wholesale to big-box retailers, $2–$5 can vanish in automatic fines for compliance mistakes.
~20%
Freight wasted on oversize cartons
When packers guess box size, they often pick one that's too big — you pay extra shipping for empty space inside the carton.
